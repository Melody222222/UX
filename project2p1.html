Improving the Online Enrollment Process for Current & Potential Parents

Project date:

Tools Used:

Role:

Researcher, UX Designer

About

Nova Pioneer Education Group is a pan - African independent school network offering preschool through secondary education for students from ages 3 through 19, and is a launchpad where ambitious young Africans can begin to shape a better future for the continent and the world.

The Issues:

I currently worked at Nova Pioneer dealing within customer service, there were moments when new parents would talk to me about their confusion on our application and enrollment process.
Parents found it time consuming and we have had parents abandon the enrollment process altogether due to the confusion and the redundant steps many parents had to take when they had more than one child to enrol. The information for enrollment and fees was not in a central area and parents would spend a lot of time trying to locate fee information and completing the application form. Parents were not given a clear understanding on the steps this process involved which left users confused and frustrated.
There are also no shortened application options for parents who currently already had children at Nova Pioneer, which meant current parents had to go through this process again too when enrolling additional children.

The Challenge

Since most parents only need to go through this process a handful of times, it did prove challenging to gather feedback without having to contact past parents who have long since forgotten their experience.

I also wanted to be mindful of not wanting to get in touch with parents for feedback. Parents are inundated with communication from the School regarding internal processes, finance related queries, school events and other school related updates such as newsletters. It was preferred to not engage our parents any further with communication and not overwhelm them with requests to complete surveys or for feedback.

I did have access to a team who help parents enrol daily. They did notice a collective theme in concerns parents share, which corroborated the data that we had on hand from usability tests and from our experience in the finance team from what parents have previously shared. In addition to this, I had access to our customer query logs to consider any other areas of concern for new parents, which would be addressed at a later stage as this case study is on-going.

Defining Users Experiences

An Affinity Workshop was done with team members remotely, using the data obtained to create a customer journey map and user flow diagram highlighted pain points for parents that could be improved going forward.

The biggest Issue Highlighted from this are:

I applied all the data to a customer journey map and created user flow diagrams to pinpoint areas to improve, clarifing the exact steps that need to be refined and remedied to remove lessen frustration for our parents.

Sketching & Prototyping
Sketching a rough interaction design for desktop booking process and finding ways to make this process a better experience for a user.
Results
Our solution focused on the following:
  • Giving parents clear instructions on the enrollment process at the very beginning and within their eyeline so this would not be missed by parents.
  • Removing fields that were deemed unnecessary from the admissions team to shorten this process.
  • Lessening this process for current families that have already enrolled previously.
  • Allowed parents to add additional students without needing to complete the guardian information again.
  • A navigation bar was recommended on the homepage to ensure parents have a clear place to find information such as the fee guideline.

    • This case study is on-going, and is part of a much larger enrollment process, as changes are made this case study will evolve as I work with our Marketing and Admissions team to continue to make positive changes on this user experience for our parents.
